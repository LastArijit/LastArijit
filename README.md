## About

I work at the intersection of raw data and structured understanding, focusing on transforming unorganized, real-world datasets into clean, reliable, and analysis-ready systems. My experience lies in working with financial and alternative asset data, where precision, consistency, and interpretation are essential.

Over time, I have developed a strong foundation in SQL, Python, and Excel, not just as tools, but as systems for thinking through data problems. My work involves cleaning and validating datasets, designing structured schemas, and building workflows that improve both efficiency and data quality. I have also contributed to process improvements by analyzing recurring data issues and implementing solutions that enhance accuracy and consistency.

Alongside professional work, I build independent projects that reflect complete data workflows — from ingestion and storage to transformation and visualization. I approach each project with a focus on clarity, reproducibility, and practical relevance, aiming to create systems that are both functional and insightful.

Currently, I am developing a **Sleep Tracking System**, where I am designing a relational database to model sleep sessions, cycles, and physiological stages. This project explores how structured data can be used to understand behavioral patterns over time, combining data modeling with analytical thinking.
